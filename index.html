Describe the data structure you used to calculate the word frequencies. Why did you choose that data structure? What benefits does your selected data structure offer?
The data structure used to calculate the word frequencies is a hash map and it is a map interface in which it stores data in (Key, Value) pairs. This data structure would make searching keys faster. The advantages of hash maps is that it internally uses a link list to store key-value pairs and will do inserts and searchs no matter the size of the map.

Was the song ever a billboard top hit? When?
"Closer" debuted at number nine on the Billboard Hot 100 in the issue dated August 20, 2016. It became the third top ten single for The Chainsmokers on the chart, after "Roses" and "Don't Let Me Down". It is Halsey's first top ten on the chart and her second top forty.

What were the top words found? Was it a surprise?
The words with the highest occurrences altogether come out as " ain't never getting older". I don't consider this as a surprise because many songs have the titles their songs after the most occurring word, but in this case it is titled with meaning behind the song "Closer".
